About/CV

Jacqueline W. Nuzzo (b. 1995) is an artist working in printmaking, drawing, and textiles. Her practice disrupts patterns and questions how repetition informs the aesethetic experience.

Most of her work uses the concept of the repeat tile, a square that creates a seamless design once placed alongside itself. Using the grid as a foundation for organization, Nuzzo disrupts that foundation by making the perfect, imperfect. Subtle variations in the repeat tile create discontinuity annd challenge the legibility within the the visual information.

For Nuzzo, fabric is an alternate substrate to paper. Stitching fabric tiles creates a feeling of tension that does not come with paper. The color of the fabric can be made through hand-dyeing techniques that are a mix of intentional and intuitive, and Nuzzo's gravitation towards subtle tonal variations of the primary color palette can be seen throughout her works.

Jacqueline W. Nuzzo graduated from St. Olaf College in 2017 with a BA in Studio Art and Biology. She is currently an MFA candidate in Printmedia at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.
